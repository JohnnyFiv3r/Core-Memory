## 1. Purpose

Dreamer is Core Memory's scientific continuity engine. It studies grounded memory
structures, storylines, overlays, goals, tensions, SOUL files,
retrieval/myelination signals, and prior Dreamer outputs to generate falsifiable
hypotheses about what has persisted, what is changing, what tensions recur, what
goals appear active or latent, what identity divergences exist, and what future
continuations are implied by existing storylines.

Dreamer does not modify grounded memory and does not author SOUL files. It
observes, measures, infers, projects, and proposes.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Dreamer is a scientist. It is not a planner, a goal authority, a forecasting
engine, or a memory writer. It produces structured findings that can be
inspected, approved, rejected, ignored, or used by the agent when maintaining
SOUL files.

---

## 3. Relationship To Existing Core Memory

Dreamer V3 integrates with existing primitives rather than inventing parallel
ones: beads, claims, C/B/A, grounding, approval status, associations, goal beads,
derived worldlines, storylines, storyline overlays, Dreamer candidates,
decide/accept/reject workflows, and myelination/retrieval-value signals. V3
extends these surfaces without violating their boundaries.

---

## 4. Relationship To Storylines

Dreamer operates primarily over storylines, where `storyline = backbone + overlay`.

### 4.1 Backbone
Grounded history, derived from existing structures (claim chains, entity
appearances, goal arcs). Answers "what happened?" Evidence-backed read-side
projection; not generated by Dreamer; not canonical memory.

### 4.2 Overlay
Accepted interpretation. Answers "what might this mean?" Interpretive, versioned,
append-only, falsifiable, traceable. Not beads, claims, or associations.

### 4.3 One-Way Rule
Interpretation must never launder into history. Overlays must never become inputs
to backbone derivation. Dreamer outputs become overlays only through an explicit
decide/accept flow. Dreamer findings must not create beads, associations, or
claims by default.

---

## 5. Current Implementation Alignment

Core Memory implements `GET /v1/memory/projection/storylines`, combining
worldline backbones, active overlays, superseded overlays, and computed tensions.
Dreamer V3 consumes this projection as its primary high-level input. It does not
re-create a worldline product layer — worldlines remain backbone internals;
storylines are the product primitive.

### 5.1 Inherited (shipped) slice — preserve
From dreamer-v2 (already in `master`): `schema/storyline_overlay.py`,
`runtime/dreamer/convergence.py` (deterministic worldline-convergence detector;
threshold-gated `narrative_candidate` emission wired into the `dreamer-run` job),
accept-path materialization to `.beads/overlays.jsonl`, `graph/storylines.py` +
`derive_storylines` + the storylines projection, and the one-way backbone
invariant under test. **V3 must keep all of this intact**; existing candidate
types and decide flows must continue to work.

## 6. Inputs

Storyline projections (backbones, active/superseded overlays, computed tensions);
SOUL files; goal beads; claims; C/B/A; grounding; human approvals/rejections;
association graph; retrieval feedback; myelination/path-use data; prior Dreamer
candidates/projections/decisions. Dreamer reads the current SOUL files every run
(SOUL is maintained theory, not an append-only log).

  `.dreamer/candidates.json`, `findings.jsonl`, `projections.jsonl`, `runs.jsonl`.

`narrative_candidate`, `goal_candidate`, `tension_candidate`, `identity_candidate`,

---

## 9. Epistemic Boundaries

Dreamer operates in the observed and inferred layers. It cannot create endorsed
truth, Goal Beads, A-class records, or directly approve goals. It produces
hypotheses and candidates; human approval or approved governance workflows are
required for endorsement.

---

## 10. Goal Bead Boundary

Goal beads are authoritative goal events (human declaration/approval/rejection/
correction, explicit completion/abandonment, authorized system-of-record event).
Dreamer may infer a `goal_candidate`; it must not create a Goal Bead from
behavioral inference.
- Valid: `goal_candidate: "Explainability appears to be a latent goal."`
- Invalid: `goal_bead: "The user has goal: explainability."`

---

## 11. Persistence

Persistence does not mean old. Persistence means **distributed recurrence across
meaningful separation** — across time, sessions, storylines, contexts, decisions,
goal references, human interactions, and evidence types. Favor distributed
recurrence over age.

### 11.1 Anti-patterns
Do not over-score persistence from one old event, one long session, one user
repetition loop, one temporary crisis, one repeated phrase, one narrow active
storyline, a superseded claim chain, speculative overlays, or agent-generated
repetition.

---

## 12. Assembly Depth

Assembly Depth is Dreamer's primary measurement primitive: an estimate of
**historical irreducibility** — how much continuity is required to explain a
structure. Eligible targets: storylines, tensions, goals, identity traits,
values, future projections, attractors. It is not age, raw frequency, or
confidence.

### 12.1 Candidate factors
`distinct_session_count`, `distinct_time_window_count`, `supporting_bead_count`,
`supporting_storyline_count`, `supporting_goal_bead_count`,
`supporting_claim_count`, `human_confirmation_count`,
`recurrence_across_contexts`, `recurrence_across_sources`,
`causal_dependency_count`, `tension_recurrence_count`, `storyline_span`,
`overlay_revision_count`, `supersession_survival`, `myelinated_path_support`,
`retrieval_feedback_support`, `kind_diversity`.

### 12.2 Anti-factors (reduce depth)
single-session concentration; single-author repetition loop; transient crisis
spike; recently superseded evidence; low-confidence evidence; speculative-only
support; contradiction by A-class evidence; lack of cross-context recurrence;
agent-output self-loop; duplicate evidence; overlay-only evidence.

### 12.3 Interpretation
High depth = historically persistent, hard to explain away, structurally
important, likely identity-relevant. Low depth = recent, local, transient,
weakly supported.

### 12.4 v1 aggregation formula

Deterministic for a fixed store state, mirroring the continuity-depth
normalization from the superseded myelination-v2 design:

```
For target T:
  raw_f      = each factor (§12.1) counted from the graph
  norm_f     = percentile-rank of raw_f within the population of comparable targets
  score+     = Σ_f ( w_f · norm_f )            # weighted sum of normalized factors
  penalty    = Σ_a ( p_a · anti_a )            # §12.2 anti-factors, normalized
  depth      = clamp01( score+ − penalty )
```

- Weights `w_f` and penalties `p_a` are **env-tunable with documented defaults**;
  v1 ships a default weighting biased toward cross-context recurrence and human
  confirmation, and away from single-session/agent-loop signals.
- The report carries the per-factor breakdown (`components`) for explainability,
  never just the scalar.
- v1 does not use Assembly Depth as a retrieval ranking term — it is a
  measurement/projection only, so consumers don't fork formulas.

---

## 13. Tension Discovery

A tension is a persistent conflict between goals, constraints, values, behaviors,
or storyline continuations. The storyline projection already computes some
tensions (competing overlays, claim-slot conflicts). V3 extends detection to goal
conflict, observed-vs-endorsed identity conflict, storyline-continuation
conflict, value/behavior conflict, and strategy/constraint conflict. Dreamer
emits `tension_candidate`s; they are not endorsed tensions until accepted into
SOUL or materialized through an approved overlay/update flow.

---

## 14. Goal Discovery

Dreamer may propose `goal_candidate`s (repeated behavior suggesting a latent
goal) but cannot make a goal authoritative. Candidates surface for human
approval, SOUL consideration, agent context, or UI review. Future projections
must not create goals.

---

## 15. Identity and Value Research

Dreamer compares observed self, endorsed self, emergent values, aspirational
values, behavior, storylines, goals, and tensions — looking for identity
divergence, values revealed/contradicted by behavior, endorsed-identity drift,
unacknowledged attractors, and repeated mismatch between stated goals and pursued
outcomes. Values remain emergent and belong in `IDENTITY.md`, not a separate
`VALUES.md`.

---

## 16. Storyline Projection

Dreamer extends existing storylines into fuzzy possible continuations. It does
not predict a single future; it generates an array of possible future vectors,
each a plausible continuation grounded in backbone continuity, overlays, goals,
tensions, Assembly Depth, current SOUL state, myelinated pathways, retrieval
feedback, and human approvals/rejections.

### 16.1 Geometry / continuity projection (migrated here, deferred — Phase 3)

The per-structure continuity-geometry export (the data layer behind a memory
visualization — radial "continuity geometry" rendering, mass/wells/curvature
readouts from `docs/reports/emergent-geometry-substrate.md`) is **owned by
Dreamer V3** as a read-only projection surface, since Dreamer now owns the depth
measure. It is **deferred to Phase 3** and is **not** on the v1 critical path:
SOUL, scientific findings, and reinforcement do not depend on it. A host builds
the geometry view only if/when it wants a memory-explorer surface; the projection
exists to serve it without forking formulas. Proposed surface:
`GET /v1/dreamer/geometry` (with compatibility alias
`GET /v1/memory/projection/geometry`) serves beads with id, type, status,
assembly_depth, title, created_at/timestamp, and entities; edges include src,
dst, rel, strength, provenance. The enriched node shape is
`dreamer_geometry_manifest.v2` / `geometry_node.v2`; persisted pre-enrichment
v1 manifests remain readable and are returned with `legacy_node_shape=true`
until a Dreamer run rebuilds them. The endpoint is served from a manifest, not
recomputed on read, and returns `present=false` until a Dreamer run has built the
manifest.

---

## 17. Future Vectors

A future vector is a projected continuation of a storyline — not a goal, not a
prediction, but a structured anticipatory hypothesis answering: *if this
storyline continues without intervention, where does it appear to point?*

### 17.1 Fields
`id`, `schema`, `source_storyline_id`, `source_backbone_id`, `current_state`,
`projected_state`, `narrative_statement`, `supporting_tensions`,
`supporting_goals`, `supporting_overlays`, `supporting_beads`,
`supporting_claims`, `assembly_depth`, `narrative_strength`, `attractor_strength`,
`intervention_sensitivity`, `expected_revision_triggers`, `confidence`,
`uncertainty_notes`, `governance_flags`.

---

## 18. Narratively Most Likely Path

Dreamer may identify one future vector as the narratively most likely path — not
statistically most probable, but the continuation requiring the fewest
explanatory jumps from the current storyline. It is informational to the user,
behaviorally relevant to the agent, and useful for "bargaining with the future."
It may influence how existing goals are pursued; it must not create goals.

---

## 19. Narrative Strength

Measures how naturally a continuation follows from an existing storyline (not
probability): storyline continuity, goal alignment, tension continuity, Assembly
Depth support, overlay support, C/B/A quality, grounding quality, absence of
contradictions, low explanatory-jump count, support from myelinated paths.

### 19.1 Edge cases
Must not be inflated by repeated phrasing from one user, temporary crisis,
recently superseded evidence, dominance of one narrative, speculative overlays,
circular support from prior Dreamer findings, self-reinforcing agent behavior,
collapsed unevaluated alternatives, or projection based only on overlays or
low-confidence C-class evidence. A continuation can be narratively strong and
still undesirable or wrong.

---

## 20. Attractor Strength

Measures how many goals, tensions, storylines, and future vectors converge toward
a projected future state — distinct from narrative strength. A future may have
high narrative / low attractor strength, or vice versa, or high attractor
strength with negative desirability. Attractor strength does not imply approval,
desirability, or goals; it only indicates convergence.

### 20.1 Edge cases
Must not be inflated by many storylines sharing one root constraint; duplicated
evidence; crisis-induced convergence; correlated-but-not-independent storylines;
agent-generated narrative loops; repeated overlays over the same backbone;
shallow goal alignment; unsupported optimism; repeated references from one
session; or the same evidence reaching the target through multiple derived paths.
A strong attractor may be a warning, not a recommendation.

---

## 21. Bargaining With The Future

A projection indicates where the current storyline points without intervention.
The agent may use this to adjust how endorsed goals are pursued — e.g., if a
future vector suggests a current strategy leads toward enterprise complexity but
the endorsed goal is operator simplicity, the agent may warn the user, suggest a
different path, prioritize simpler routes, surface the tension, or propose a SOUL
update. Projections influence pursuit; they do not create pursuit.

---

## 22. Projection Governance

Future projections are advisory and contextual. They may be shown to users, used
as agent context, used to inform SOUL proposals, used to highlight risks, used to
adjust pursuit of existing goals, and used to generate `future_projection_candidate`
rows. They may not create or approve goals, override human intent, mutate
backbones/beads/claims, become backbone evidence, or become overlays without a
decide/accept flow.

---

## 23. Storage and Materialization

Dreamer outputs remain separate from grounded evidence.
- Existing candidate flow at `.beads/events/dreamer-candidates.json` stays
  intact; accepted narrative candidates may materialize as storyline overlays.
- Future projections store initially as `.beads/events/dreamer-projections.jsonl`
  — not as beads, claims, associations, or overlays by default.
- Only accepted historical-interpretation candidates become storyline overlays.
  Projection candidates may reference overlays but do not become overlays unless
  a future overlay schema is explicitly introduced and approved.

## 26. Evaluation Requirements

Tests: backbones unchanged when projections exist; overlays excluded from
backbone derivation; projection records create no beads/claims/associations;
narrative strength penalizes single-session repetition and superseded evidence;
attractor strength penalizes duplicated evidence and distinguishes convergence
from desirability; goal candidates do not create Goal Beads; future projections
do not create goals; SOUL files are read but never written by Dreamer; Assembly
Depth penalizes agent-output self-loops and is deterministic for a fixed store.

---

## 27. Success Criteria

Identifies persistent tensions; measures Assembly Depth without equating
persistence with age; produces useful storyline projections and future vectors;
identifies a narratively most likely continuation; detects attractors without
treating them as goals; keeps findings outside grounded evidence; preserves the
backbone/overlay separation; supports SOUL maintenance without authoring SOUL;
helps users and agents bargain with the future.

---

## 28. Non-Goals

Does not write beads/claims/associations, create Goal Beads or endorsed truths,
directly update SOUL files, mutate storyline backbones, treat overlays as backbone
evidence, replace statistical forecasting or planning, override human approval, or
create goals from future projections.

---

## 29. Summary

Core Memory remembers. Storylines organize grounded continuity. Overlays capture
accepted interpretation. SOUL maintains the current theory of self. Dreamer
studies all of the above — measuring historical irreducibility, discovering
persistent tensions, surfacing latent goals, detecting identity divergence, and
projecting possible future continuations. Dreamer does not tell the system what
to want; it helps the system see where it is already going.

## 31. Forward look: Dreamer V4 — Target States as the primitive under goals

A future evolution (V4, not in V3 scope) treats **even human-declared goals as
observations** and abstracts beneath them to **target states**.

A goal is linguistic: *"Reduce onboarding friction."* A target state is
structural: *"A new user reaches a first successful outcome with low confusion,
low support burden, and high confidence."* The structural form matters because a
system can navigate toward a target state even when no one has perfectly named
the goal — and humans often *say* one goal while *behaving* toward another. The
gap between the two is exactly what Dreamer should detect, scientifically, rather
than convert into a goal automatically.

This is Levin-like: biological systems maintain target morphologies ("be a
two-eyed salamander," "close the wound," "restore the limb pattern") — they hold a
target *state*, not a sentence. For Core Memory, Dreamer V4 could infer target
states such as: "the organization repeatedly reduces ambiguity before scaling,"
"the user repeatedly turns tools into continuity systems," "the agent repeatedly
prefers explanations that preserve causal lineage." These are not goals — they are
inferred attractor descriptions.

### 31.1 Refined primitive hierarchy

```
Observed behavior
  ↓
Implied target state      (Dreamer V4 infers)
  ↓
Goal candidate            (Dreamer surfaces)
  ↓
Human-endorsed goal       (SOUL endorses)
  ↓
SOUL goal hierarchy
```

### 31.2 Distinctions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Goal** | endorsed pursuit |
| **Target state** | inferred attractor — a state the system navigates toward |
| **Attractor** | a future/state exerting pull across multiple storylines |
| **Value** | a durable preference revealed by repeated target-state selection |

Target state and attractor are close kin: a target state is the *state* the
system appears to navigate toward; an attractor (V3 §20) is the convergence of
many storylines/goals/vectors onto such a state. V4 makes the target state a
first-class inferred object upstream of goals.

### 31.3 The "humans fool themselves" problem

```
Declared goal:        Move fast.
Observed target state: Avoid irreversible mistakes.
Tension:              Speed vs reversibility.
```

V4 Dreamer surfaces this as a `target_state_candidate` + a `tension_candidate`,
**scientifically** — it never auto-converts the observed target state into a goal.
SOUL may endorse a target state into a goal (the agent authors, humans anchor);
only then does the audited goal lifecycle apply.

### 31.4 Boundaries (unchanged invariants)

- **Dreamer** detects target states (observed/inferred only; new
  `target_state_candidate` family).
- **SOUL** may endorse a target state into a goal (§ SOUL goal hierarchy).
- **Myelination** never reinforces "movement toward a target state" — only
  audited paths toward approved/resolved outcomes (Myelination PRD §12.3).

V4 is recorded here so V3's `attractor_candidate` and goal-discovery work are
designed not to foreclose it; the target-state family is deliberately deferred
until V3's measurement and discovery phases land.

## 32. Research backlog: agency-layer primitives (conjecture — needs architecture)

> **Status: conjecture, not committed scope.** These are candidate primitives for
> the layer biology evolved *on top of* memory — agency, not recall. They are
> recorded so V3/V4 don't foreclose them, and each is grounded in what Core Memory
> already has so the gap is "build the projection," not "invent from scratch."
> None is designed yet; each needs refinement into real architecture before it
> becomes a PRD.

The memory side is largely covered (past continuity via worldlines/storylines,
future continuity via Dreamer projections). The gaps are agency-side. In rough
priority:

### 32.1 Counterfactual storylines (highest priority)
*Past continuity and possible-future continuity exist; **possible-past** does
not.* Humans spend enormous effort on roads not taken — "I almost took that job."
Abandoned/alternative trajectories often explain current goals, tensions, and
identity better than the realized one.
- **Already present:** the bead contrast fields `what_almost_happened`,
  `what_was_rejected`, `what_felt_risky`, `assumption`; and `abandoned_path`
  (legacy → `outcome` with `result=abandoned`). The *write-side data exists.*
- **Missing:** a **counterfactual storyline** derivation — a backbone over
  abandoned/contrast evidence, parallel to the observed backbone, sitting between
  Storylines, Dreamer, and SOUL. Completes the temporal set:
  past / present / possible-future / **possible-past**.
- This is the strongest next-primitive candidate: it reuses existing fields and
  the storyline machinery rather than new substrate.

### 32.2 Regret (informational, not emotional)
`regret ≈ expected_outcome − actual_outcome` in hindsight.
- **Already present:** `decision → outcome` chains.
- **Missing:** capturing **expected outcome** at decision time, then computing the
  expected/actual gap. A new contrast field (`expected_outcome`) + a Dreamer
  `gap_report` over resolved decisions. Feeds tension and target-state discovery.

### 32.3 Identity stability / inertia
Continuity-preserving systems need inertia — not "protect identity from truth,"
but "don't rewrite identity every week."
- **Already present:** SOUL §10 guardrails (prefer revision over deletion, small
  diffs, supersede not erase) encode the *spirit*.
- **Missing:** an explicit **identity-stability metric** (rate-of-change budget /
  hysteresis on endorsed identity) so churn is measurable and dampened.

### 32.4 Curiosity (unresolved compression error)
Tensions capture *conflict*; curiosity captures *unexplained pattern* — "we keep
seeing this and don't know why." Distinct from tension.
- **Missing:** a `curiosity_candidate` Dreamer family for recurring patterns with
  no causal explanation (high recurrence + low causal-dependency support).

### 32.5 Salience (importance prioritization)
"What deserves attention?" — without copying biology's emotion/trauma/novelty
implementation.
- **Already present (partial):** myelination (use-strength) + Assembly Depth
  (structural importance) approximate this.
- **Missing:** a unified salience readout combining depth, myelination, recency of
  open tensions, and goal-linkage to rank what Dreamer/SOUL attend to next.

### 32.6 Narrative compression beyond identity
SOUL compresses *identity*. The same compression serves project / company /
product / relationship stories.
- **Already present:** storylines; SOUL's **per-subject scoping** (§ SOUL 4.2)
  already generalizes self-narrative to non-identity subjects.
- **Missing:** narrative-compression outputs for non-identity subjects (a
  story-synthesis projection per subject), distinct from the self-model.

### 32.7 Self-prediction (future self state)
Dreamer projects future *world* (storyline future vectors). It does not project
future **self** — "if I take this job I'll burn out."
- **Missing:** a SOUL-adjacent **future-self-state** projection: apply Dreamer's
  future-vector machinery to the SOUL self-model, not just external storylines.

### 32.8 Meta-goals (goal governance)
Goals about goals — "I want better goals," "I want to stop wanting this." A layer
asking *should we still want this?*
- **Already present (partial):** SOUL governance modes (§9), goal decay (§12),
  Dreamer `goal_decay_warning`.
- **Missing:** an explicit **goal-governance** layer where Dreamer participates,
  humans approve, and SOUL records meta-goals as first-class — distinct from the
  object-level goal hierarchy.

**Temporal completeness as the organizing frame:** with counterfactual storylines
(32.1) and self-prediction (32.7), the system would reason over
`past · present · possible-future · possible-past` for both world and self — the
four trajectories humans actually use. That framing, not any single feature, is
the argument for prioritizing counterfactuals next.
